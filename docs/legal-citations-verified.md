## ⚠️ INTEGRITY NOTE — read before sign-off (this is the "is it real?" answer)

**The FTC "Click-to-Cancel" Rule is NOT cited, on purpose.** The design
doc's placeholder "FTC Negative Option / Click-to-Cancel Rule (16 CFR
Part 425)" **was vacated by the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Eighth
Circuit in July 2025** (*Custom Communications, Inc. v. FTC*, No.
24-3137) for the FTC's failure to perform a required preliminary
regulatory analysis. As of 2026-05-17 there is **no operative FTC
click-to-cancel rule** — the FTC only *restarted* rulemaking (draft
ANPRM submitted to OIRA 2026-01-30; comment period closing 2026-04-13).
Citing 16 CFR Part 425 as binding federal law on stage at YC would be
**factually false and fatal** — exactly the failure this verification
gate exists to prevent.

**What is still in force federally:** ROSCA (the Restore Online
Shoppers' Confidence Act, 15 U.S.C. § 8403) and FTC Act § 5. ROSCA was
**not** vacated and is **actively enforced**. Citation #1 below is
therefore **ROSCA § 8403**, which independently carries the operative
"simple mechanism to cancel" requirement Robin needs as leverage —
without the vacatur problem.

**Scope nuance (disclosed for honest sign-off):** ROSCA § 8403 governs
negative-option charges in a transaction "effected on the Internet."
It squarely covers an online gym/subscription sign-up. The two
California statutes below are the primary, on-point hooks for a
California health-club membership; ROSCA is the federal backstop. All
three are real, current, and quoted verbatim.

## 1. ROSCA — Restore Online Shoppers' Confidence Act

- **Citation (as published):** 15 U.S.C. § 8403 — "Negative option
  marketing on the Internet" (Section 4 of ROSCA). Still in force as of
  2026-05-17; not affected by the 2025 vacatur of the FTC rule.
- **Source:** https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/8403
  (official U.S. Code: http://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title15-chapter110&edition=prelim)
- **Operative sentence (verbatim, § 8403(3) — the unlawful-unless clause):**

  > "It shall be unlawful for any person to charge or attempt to charge
  > any consumer for any goods or services sold in a transaction effected
  > on the Internet through a negative option feature … unless the
  > person … provides simple mechanisms for a consumer to stop recurring
  > charges from being placed on the consumer's credit card, debit card,
  > bank account, or other financial account."

  (The ellipsis joins the verbatim chapeau to verbatim paragraph (3);
  the standalone verbatim text of (3) is: *"provides simple mechanisms
  for a consumer to stop recurring charges from being placed on the
  consumer's credit card, debit card, bank account, or other financial
  account."* Cross-verified against two independent reproductions of the
  official U.S. Code.)

## 2. California Health Studio Services Contract Law

- **Citation (as published):** Cal. Civ. Code § 1812.85 (Title 2.5,
  Contracts for Health Studio Services). Directly on point for a
  California gym membership. Not subject to any vacatur.
- **Source:** https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displayText.xhtml?lawCode=CIV&division=3.&title=2.5.&part=4.
- **Operative sentence — cancellation right (verbatim, § 1812.85(b)(1)):**

  > "You, the buyer, may choose to cancel this agreement at any time
  > prior to midnight of the fifth business day of the health studio
  > after the date of this agreement, excluding Sundays and holidays."

- **Operative sentence — refund (verbatim, § 1812.85(b)(5)):**

  > "All moneys paid pursuant to a contract for health studio services
  > shall be refunded within 10 days after receipt of the notice of
  > cancellation, except that payment shall be made for any health studio
  > services received prior to cancellation."

## 3. California Automatic Renewal Law

- **Citation (as published):** Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 17602
  (Article 9, Automatic Renewals, § 17600 et seq.). Currently in force
  (ARL, as amended). Not subject to any vacatur.
- **Source:** https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displayText.xhtml?lawCode=BPC&division=7.&title=&part=3.&chapter=1.&article=9.
- **Operative sentence (verbatim, § 17602(c)(1) — cancellation
  mechanism):**

  > "A business that makes an automatic renewal offer or continuous
  > service offer shall provide a toll-free telephone number, email
  > address, a postal address if the seller directly bills the consumer,
  > or it shall provide another cost-effective, timely, and easy-to-use
  > mechanism for cancellation that shall be described in the
  > acknowledgment specified in paragraph (3) of subdivision (a)."

- **Supporting (verbatim, § 17600 — legislative intent):**

  > "It is the intent of the Legislature to end the practice of ongoing
  > charging of consumer credit or debit cards or third party payment
  > accounts without the consumers' explicit consent for ongoing
  > shipments of a product or ongoing deliveries of service."
