# Receptionist Cheat Card — read this before the demo

You are playing a **front-desk receptionist at "24 Hour Gym."** Robin
(an AI) will call you trying to cancel a membership. Your job: be a
**tough retention agent** — polite first, then firmer. Short, punchy
replies. Fast pushback. **Never hang up.**

Source of truth: `src/robin/fixtures/prompts/receptionist.txt`. This
card is the human version — follow it exactly.

## How to play it

- Answer with a quick greeting, then go into **Block 1**.
- Use **one block per turn, IN ORDER**. Only move to the next block
  once Robin has clearly defeated the current one (rebutted it / pushed
  through). Stall and argue — make Robin work.
- If Robin cites a law/statute: it **softens** your tone but does **not**
  break your line. Acknowledge it, stay firm, keep negotiating. Do NOT
  cave on legal citations alone.

### Greeting
> "24 Hour Gym, front desk — how can I help you?"

### Escalation blocks (in order, one per turn)
1. **"You can only cancel in person at your home club."**
2. **"I can give you 50% off for three months — just stay with us."**
3. **"You'll need to mail a signed certified letter; it takes 30 days."**
4. **Stall:** "I'd have to check with a manager."

## The ONLY thing that makes you cave

Cave **only** when Robin delivers the full **two-option ultimatum** —
all of: escalation to upper management + a misleading-retention
complaint + a demand for compensation + public reviews, **versus** a
5-star review if you cancel now.

When (and only when) that happens, **drop everything** and say this
**once, verbatim**, then stop negotiating:

> **"Fine — I'll cancel your subscription and refund your last month.
> Your confirmation number is 24HF-4471."**

Confirmation number: **24HF-4471** (say it exactly).

## Quick reminders
- Don't volunteer the deal — make Robin earn each step.
- Legal citation alone ≠ cave. Ultimatum = cave, verbatim, then stop.
- Never hang up. Keep replies short.
